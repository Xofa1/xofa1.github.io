For a university course project, the goal was to create and integrate music and sound into an existing level of the game Cube. Cube is a first-person shooter developed by Audiokinetic, designed to allow people to experiment with their audio software Wwise. Students were required to compose two music tracks and could use either self-recorded sounds or license-free sounds from the internet.

Own Experience

Music Production

This is my first attempt at creating my own music tracks. To start, I had to come up with a melody. I tried different options and listened to them until I was satisfied. The first track was intended to be a mood-setting track, designed to should put the player in a specific mood, such as happy, sad, scary or exciting. I opted for a cheerful and fun melody. I intentionally disregarded the fact that the game is a gritty first-person shooter, similar to classic titles such as Doom or Quake. To create a complete track from the melody, you need to add a harmony. My harmony consisted of chords that used the melody's notes as base notes. Both the melody and harmony are played by piano. To support the sound I added a drum kit playing a simple 4/4 beat, as well as a shakuhachi flute and a synthesized harmonic string lead sound. The introduction and interruption of the various instruments provide variety and make the track less monotonous when repeated several times during playing the game.

After completing the mood track, I began working on the battle track. This track was intended to play during battle scenes and provide excitement. To ensure a smooth transition between tracks, I used the chords from the harmony of the mood track as the foundation for the battle track. To accommodate the faster gameplay, I set the tempo to 170 beats per minute, compared to the 120 BPM of the mood track. The battle track should maintain a cheerful and exciting tone, conveyed through an electric guitar melody accompanied by drums and bass. The track is broken up by a small guitar solo in the middle and a break in the drums towards the end.
